# PT02.002 — Automated Social Proofing

## Metadata

---

## Description

Using botnets or AI-driven personas to interact with an adversary-controlled "seed" persona on LinkedIn, X, or GitHub. By generating fake endorsements, comments, and repository "Stars," the adversary builds a high-trust digital footprint that bypasses manual and automated "fake profile" checks by HR or Security teams. This manufactured legitimacy significantly increases the success rate of downstream spear-phishing and social engineering operations.

---

## Adversary Value

- Increases the success rate of spear-phishing or social engineering by 4–5× because the target sees a "vouched for" professional profile
- Bypasses automated fake-profile detection systems that rely on engagement metrics as trust signals
- Enables the adversary's synthetic or real identity to pass HR pre-employment screening and vendor security assessments
- GitHub star counts and LinkedIn endorsements are widely treated as credibility signals by both humans and automated screening tools

---

## Why It's Not in MITRE ATT&CK

MITRE does not account for the **industrialisation of digital legitimacy** — the systematic, automated manufacture of social proof across professional platforms. While MITRE covers account establishment, it treats identity credibility as a binary (real/fake) rather than as a spectrum that can be actively manipulated through coordinated inauthentic behaviour.

---

## Procedure Examples

1. Deploy a botnet or network of AI-managed personas seeded across LinkedIn, X, and GitHub
2. Orchestrate coordinated engagement with the target "seed" persona: endorsements, skill validations, post interactions, and repository stars
3. Establish mutual connections with real professionals in the target industry to increase network credibility
4. Allow the manufactured legitimacy to accumulate for 60–90 days before leveraging the persona in a targeting operation
5. Use the high-trust persona to initiate contact with targets, referencing the visible social proof as a credibility anchor

---

## Countermeasures

### Prevention

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **Referral Verification Rigor** | For any inbound contact that passes initial screening, require at least one verifiable, independent human referral — not just "mutual connections" or endorsements visible on public profiles. |
| **Vendor/Contractor Onboarding Hardening** | Implement a "trust cooldown" period for new external contacts — no access to sensitive systems or data for the first 30 days, with escalating trust based on verified interactions. |
| **GitHub/GitLab Star-Count Scepticism** | When evaluating open-source dependencies or contractor portfolios, weigh commit history quality over vanity metrics (stars, forks, endorsements). |

### Detection

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **Botnet-Pattern Analysis on Social Platforms** | Monitor for coordinated inauthentic behaviour (CIB) patterns — clusters of profiles that all engage with the same "seed" persona within a short time window. |
| **Social Graph Anomaly Detection** | Flag profiles where the ratio of endorsements-to-actual-professional-interactions is abnormally high. |

### Response

- Block and report the seed persona and all associated bot accounts on the respective social platforms
- Conduct a post-mortem on whether the adversary's social proofing succeeded in any phishing or social engineering attempts against your organisation
