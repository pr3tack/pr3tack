# PT18.001 — Holiday/Travel Window Triggering

## Metadata

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **ID** | PT18.001 |
| **Tactic** | PT18 — Operational Tempo Manipulation |
| **MITRE Gap** | Not mapped in MITRE ATT&CK. | **Response Priority** | High |

---

## Description

An adversary deliberately times the execution of attack operations to coincide with periods of known reduced security team staffing — public holidays, major travel windows (Christmas/New Year, Thanksgiving, Eid, Diwali), organisational all-hands events, or after-hours periods. During these windows, SOC coverage is reduced, incident response times are slower, and escalation paths are degraded — significantly extending the adversary's available dwell time and reducing the probability of effective containment. This technique is a force multiplier that does not require any additional technical capability.

---

## Adversary Value

- Achieves longer dwell time without any additional technical evasion — reduced coverage extends the exploitation window naturally
- Slows incident response: on-call responders have degraded tool access, communication overhead increases, and decision-making authority is often unclear during holiday periods
- Consistent and predictable — public holidays are known months in advance, enabling precise operational planning
- Particularly effective against small security teams where holiday coverage is a single point of failure

---

## Why It's Not in MITRE ATT&CK

This technique has no MITRE mapping. MITRE ATT&CK focuses on technical techniques against systems. Operational timing is a strategic adversarial consideration that exploits organisational and human factors rather than technical controls. The consistent pattern of major attacks occurring during holiday periods (NotPetya on Ukrainian Constitution Day, many ransomware attacks on US holiday weekends) validates this as a real and systematic adversarial technique.

---

## Procedure Examples

1. Adversary identifies the target organisation's operational calendar through open-source intelligence (public holiday schedules, company blog posts about team events, LinkedIn posts about all-hands meetings)
2. Pre-positions access, tools, and payloads in advance of the target window (weeks or months prior)
3. Executes the primary attack operation on a Friday afternoon before a major public holiday weekend
4. Takes advantage of the 48–72 hour reduced-coverage window to achieve objectives (data exfiltration, ransomware deployment, persistent access establishment) before Monday morning triage
5. Uses the slow initial response to deepen access and pre-empt containment actions

---

## Countermeasures

### Prevention

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **24/7 SOC coverage** | Ensure consistent SOC coverage during holidays and high-risk periods — consider outsourcing to an MSSP for holiday coverage if internal capacity is insufficient |
| **Pre-holiday threat hunting** | Conduct proactive threat hunts in the week before major holidays; look for pre-positioned access, unusual dormant processes, and new persistence mechanisms |
| **Incident response readiness drills** | Conduct tabletop exercises specifically for holiday-period scenarios; ensure on-call responders have pre-staged tooling and clear escalation paths |

### Detection

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **Holiday-period anomaly alerting** | Increase alert sensitivity thresholds during holiday periods — lower thresholds for investigation-triggering events to compensate for reduced analyst capacity |
| **Pre-holiday access pattern changes** | Alert on new persistence mechanisms, new scheduled tasks, or new authorised access grants in the week preceding major holidays |
| **After-hours privilege use** | Alert on use of privileged accounts or administrative access outside business hours, particularly in the 72-hour window surrounding public holidays |

### Response

- Activate full incident response capacity immediately — do not delay response due to holiday staffing
- Engage MSSP or IR retainer if internal capacity is degraded
- Prioritise containment over investigation during the initial response window — prevent deepening of access before full team is available
- Conduct a retrospective analysis of pre-holiday activity in the week before the attack for pre-positioning evidence
