# PT16.002 — Procurement Account Establishment

## Metadata

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **ID** | PT16.002 |
| **Tactic** | PT16 — Governance Subversion |
| **MITRE Gap** | MITRE T1585 (Establish Accounts) covers account creation for operational use; this technique specifically describes establishing a fraudulent **vendor identity** in a target organisation's procurement and vendor management system — gaining trusted vendor status to access procurement data, insert into supply chains, or establish legitimate-looking invoicing relationships for financial fraud or data access |
| **Response Priority** | Medium |
| **Status** | Draft |

---

## Description

An adversary creates a fraudulent vendor or supplier identity — complete with a registered business entity, banking details, professional website, and fabricated references — and successfully registers it in the target organisation's vendor management or procurement system. Once registered as an approved vendor, the adversary gains access to vendor portals, procurement data, RFP documents, and internal contact lists. This vendor identity can also be used to submit fraudulent invoices (business email compromise variant), insert malicious hardware into purchase orders, or establish a trusted relationship for social engineering.

---

## Adversary Value

- Approved vendor status provides access to vendor portals that may contain sensitive procurement data, internal project details, and contact directories
- Fraudulent invoicing against a registered vendor account is a reliable financial fraud vector — payment teams trust registered vendor accounts
- Vendor relationships provide legitimate pretexts for follow-on social engineering (product support calls, account management interactions)
- Supply chain insertion: as an approved vendor, the adversary can submit hardware or software into legitimate purchase orders

---

## Why It's Not in MITRE ATT&CK

MITRE T1585 covers account creation for infrastructure and operational personas. This technique is distinct — it describes establishing a fraudulent **vendor business identity** within a target's formal procurement governance process, exploiting the trust model of third-party vendor management rather than creating operational accounts for direct attack use.

---

## Procedure Examples

1. Adversary registers a shell company with a plausible name similar to a legitimate vendor (e.g., "Acme IT Solutions Ltd" vs the legitimate "Acme IT Services Ltd")
2. Creates a professional website, LinkedIn presence, and fabricated client references for the shell company
3. Submits a vendor registration application to the target's procurement portal, passing KYC/due diligence checks using the fabricated business identity
4. Once approved, gains access to the vendor portal — extracting RFP documents, procurement calendars, and internal contact details
5. Submits fraudulent invoices for services not rendered, or uses the vendor relationship as a trusted pretext for spear phishing of finance and procurement staff

---

## Countermeasures

### Prevention

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **Enhanced vendor due diligence** | Require independent verification of vendor registration numbers, banking details (via confirmed bank letter), and at least two independently-verified client references |
| **Vendor portal access restriction** | Restrict vendor portal access to the minimum information necessary for the vendor relationship; do not expose procurement calendars or internal contact directories |
| **Invoice validation controls** | Require dual-approval for new vendor invoices; validate bank account details against the original registration independently before first payment |

### Detection

| Control | Description |
|---|---|
| **Vendor name similarity screening** | Screen new vendor registrations for names with high similarity to existing approved vendors (edit distance ≤ 2) |
| **New vendor bank account change monitoring** | Alert on changes to bank account details for existing approved vendors — a common BEC pattern |
| **Vendor portal access anomaly** | Alert on new vendor accounts accessing document types or portal sections beyond their expected scope |

### Response

- Immediately suspend the fraudulent vendor account and revoke portal access
- Claw back any fraudulent payments through the banking channel (contact your bank's fraud team within 24 hours for best recovery odds)
- Notify law enforcement and the relevant company registrar
- Conduct a full audit of all invoices and payments to the fraudulent vendor account
- Review vendor due diligence controls and tighten KYC requirements
