# Hunt: SaaS App-to-App Permission Mining — OAuth Scope Enumeration

---

## Hypothesis

An adversary is systematically enumerating OAuth permission scopes, app-to-app trust relationships, and third-party integration access grants within our SaaS environment — using legitimate API access or public marketplace visibility to map high-value permission chains before exploitation.

---

## Hunt Scope

| Scope Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Time window** | Last 30 days |
| **Data sources** | Okta system logs, Entra ID audit logs, Google Workspace Admin audit logs, OAuth consent logs, SaaS marketplace scraping telemetry |
| **Key questions** | Which OAuth apps have been granted unusually broad scopes? Are any apps requesting scopes they have never used? Which apps have access to high-value resources (email, calendar, files, admin APIs)? Are there apps with zero usage but high-privilege grants? |
| **Priority targets** | OAuth scope grants to `mail.read`, `files.readwrite.all`, `directory.read.all`, `admin.*`, `calendars.readwrite`, Google Drive full access |

---

## Hunt Queries

### Hunt 1 — Apps with High-Privilege Scopes and Low Usage

```sql
SELECT app_name, app_id, granted_scopes,
       last_used_date, grant_date,
       DATEDIFF(NOW(), last_used_date) as days_since_last_use
FROM oauth_grants
WHERE granted_scopes ILIKE '%mail.read%'
   OR granted_scopes ILIKE '%files.readwrite.all%'
   OR granted_scopes ILIKE '%directory.read.all%'
   OR granted_scopes ILIKE '%admin%'
AND (last_used_date IS NULL OR DATEDIFF(NOW(), last_used_date) > 30)
ORDER BY grant_date ASC
```

### Hunt 2 — OAuth Consent Spike — New Apps Granted Access

Hunt for unusual spikes in OAuth consent grants within a short time window — indicative of automated app enumeration or a social engineering campaign pushing users to grant permissions:

```sql
SELECT DATE(consent_timestamp) as consent_date,
       COUNT(*) as new_grants,
       COUNT(DISTINCT app_id) as distinct_apps,
       COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) as distinct_users
FROM oauth_consent_log
WHERE consent_timestamp >= NOW() - INTERVAL 30 DAYS
GROUP BY consent_date
HAVING new_grants > (SELECT AVG(daily_grants) * 3 FROM daily_grant_baseline)
ORDER BY new_grants DESC
```

### Hunt 3 — Apps Requesting Scopes Beyond Their Declared Function

Identify apps whose declared category/description does not match the scope of permissions requested — a signal for scope overprescription used in permission mining:

```sql
SELECT app_name, app_category, granted_scopes, publisher_domain
FROM oauth_grants
WHERE (app_category IN ('Productivity', 'Note-taking', 'Time tracking')
       AND (granted_scopes ILIKE '%directory%' OR granted_scopes ILIKE '%admin%'))
OR (app_category IN ('Analytics', 'Reporting')
    AND granted_scopes ILIKE '%mail%')
ORDER BY grant_date DESC
```

### Hunt 4 — Marketplace Profile Scraping Telemetry

Hunt for automated access to your SaaS platform's app marketplace or integration directory from non-browser clients:

```sql
SELECT src_ip, user_agent, COUNT(*) as request_count,
       COUNT(DISTINCT app_listing_id) as listings_viewed
FROM marketplace_access_logs
WHERE (user_agent NOT ILIKE '%mozilla%' AND user_agent NOT ILIKE '%chrome%'
       AND user_agent NOT ILIKE '%safari%')
AND timestamp >= NOW() - INTERVAL 30 DAYS
GROUP BY src_ip, user_agent
HAVING listings_viewed > 50
ORDER BY listings_viewed DESC
```

## Expected Findings

| Finding Type | Likely Explanation | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| High-privilege app grant unused for 30+ days | Abandoned over-permissioned integration — remediate | Medium |
| 3x spike in OAuth consent grants in one day | Social engineering or malicious app campaign | High |
| Analytics app with mail read scope | Scope mismatch — investigate publisher and usage | High |
| Non-browser client scraping 50+ marketplace listings | Automated permission surface mapping | High |

---

## Tuning Notes

- **App allowlisting:** Exclude known-good, reviewed integrations from Hunt 1 — focus on apps not in your approved integration registry
- **Baseline variance:** Hunt 2's spike threshold should be calibrated against your historical OAuth grant cadence — new product launches or IT migrations can cause legitimate spikes
- **Publisher verification:** For Hunt 3 findings, verify whether the publisher is a legitimate vendor with a documented reason for broad scopes before escalating

---

## Validation

1. Register a test OAuth app in your IdP with `mail.read` and `directory.read.all` scopes
2. Grant consent and leave unused for 48 hours
3. Confirm Hunt 1 identifies the app with `days_since_last_use > 1`
4. Issue 60 automated marketplace listing requests from a non-browser client
5. Confirm Hunt 4 identifies the automated client
