## Objective

Detect IOC feed pollution by tracking the false positive rate of consumed threat intelligence feeds and by monitoring for own-infrastructure listings in external threat intelligence sources.

---

## Analytic Logic

### Analytic 1 — Feed False Positive Rate Degradation

Track the false positive rate (IOCs that match legitimate internal or trusted external traffic) per threat intelligence feed source. Alert when a source's false positive rate increases significantly above its historical baseline.

```
Baseline period: 30 days per feed source
Metric: false_positive_rate = count(ioc_matches_on_trusted_traffic) / count(total_ioc_matches)
Alert: false_positive_rate_7d > false_positive_rate_30d_baseline * 3
       AND total_matches_7d > 10  # Minimum volume for statistical validity
```

### Analytic 2 — Own Infrastructure Appearing in External Threat Intelligence

Monitor for your own IP ranges, domains, and email infrastructure appearing in external threat intelligence feeds as malicious indicators — a key signal of IOC framing attacks.

```
Data source: Threat intelligence platform with own-asset monitoring
Alert: ioc_value IN (own_ip_ranges + own_domains + own_email_infrastructure)
       AND ioc_classification = 'malicious'
       AND ioc_source NOT IN (internal_feeds)
Severity: Critical
```

### Analytic 3 — IOC Source Submission Volume Spike

Alert on sudden increases in IOC submission volume from a specific source — indicative of automated pollution submission.

```
Baseline period: 30 days per submitter
Alert: submission_count_24h > submission_count_30d_daily_avg * 10
       AND submitter_reputation_score < threshold
```

---

## Tuning Notes

- Analytic 2 requires your IP ranges, domains, and email infrastructure to be configured as a monitored asset list in your TIP
- Many TIPs (ThreatConnect, MISP, Anomali) support own-asset monitoring natively
- Analytic 1 requires feedback loop integration between your SIEM blocklist matches and your TIP to track which IOCs match against trusted traffic

---

## Validation

1. Submit a test IOC matching one of your own IP addresses to a test MISP instance
2. Confirm Analytic 2 fires on the own-infrastructure listing
3. Simulate a false positive rate spike by submitting known-benign IOCs and confirm Analytic 1 fires
